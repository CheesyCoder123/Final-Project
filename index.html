Blue Whales

  • The Blue whale's scientific name is Balaenoptera musculus.
  • Their low frequency wistle can be heard for hundreds of miles and is probobly used to attract other blue whales.
  • They are the loudest animals on the planet. Their calls reach 188 dicebels, while a jet reaches 140 dicibels.
  • They weigh about 200 tons.
  • They eat about four tons of krill each day.
  • The blue whale has a heart the size of a Volkswagen Beetle.
  • They can grow to be 100 feet long. About 16 times the height of the average adult.
