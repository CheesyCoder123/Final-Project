Alligators

  • The alligators scientific name is Alligator mississippiensis
  • The alligator has a bite force of 2125 PSI.
  • The alligator has the strongest bite out of the whole animal kingdom.
  • The alligator is the biggest reptile in north africa.
  • American alligators can grow to be up to 13 feet and weight 800 pounds.
  • Did you know that alligators eat not just meat but also fruit too?
  • American alligators have been observed using lures to hunt birds.
    They balance sticks and branches on their heads, attracting birds looking for nesting material.
  • Besides swimming, alligators walk, run, and crawl on land. They have a "high walk" and a "low walk."
    The low walk is sprawling, while in the high walk the alligator lifts its belly off the ground.
  • The alligator is a devoted mom who will stay with her babies untill they are 1 year old.
  • The alligator is a vocal animal, they make a variety of different growls and hisses to warn or to protect eachoter.
